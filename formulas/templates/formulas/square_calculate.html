Description of a Square

A square is a geometric figure that is a special type of quadrilateral. It has four equal sides and four right angles (each measuring 90 degrees). Because of these properties, a square is both a rectangle (a quadrilateral with four right angles) and a rhombus (a quadrilateral with four equal sides). The diagonals of a square are equal in length and bisect each other at right angles.

Area of the square

The area of a square is calculated by squaring the length of one of its sides.
The formula for the area is: Side*Side or A*A

Perimeter of the square

The perimeter of a square is calculated by multiplying the length of one side by 4.
The formula for the perimeter is: 4*side or 4*A
