Description of a circle

A circle is a shape in which all points are equidistant from a fixed central point called the center. The distance from the center to any point on the circle is called the radius. The distance across the circle through the center is called the diameter, which is twice the length of the radius.

Designations

  • π = irrational constant
  • r = radius

Area

The area A of a circle is the space contained within its boundary.
Area = π * r2

Perimeter

The perimeter C of a circle, also known as the circumference, is the distance around the circle.
Perimeter = 2π * r
