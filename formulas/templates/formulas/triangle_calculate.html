Description of a triangle

A triangle is a geometric figure that is a polygon with three sides and three angles. The sum of the interior angles of a triangle always equals 180 degrees. Triangles are classified based on their side lengths and angles:

  • Equilateral Triangle
  • Isosceles Triangle
  • Scalene Triangle
  • Right Triangle
  • Acute Triangle
  • Obtuse Triangle

Equilateral triangle

An equilateral triangle is a special type of triangle where all three sides are of equal length, and all three interior angles are equal, each measuring 60 degrees. Because of its symmetrical properties, the equilateral triangle is considered one of the most basic and significant types of triangles in geometry.

The area of an equilateral triangle can be calculated using the formula:
Area = √3/4 * length2

The perimeter P of an equilateral triangle is simply the sum of all its sides, which can be calculated as:
Perimeter = 3 * length

Isosceles triangle

An isosceles triangle is a type of triangle that has two sides of equal length. The angles opposite these equal sides are also equal. This symmetry gives the isosceles triangle some unique properties and makes it a fundamental shape in geometry.
a = side
b = base

The area A of an isosceles triangle can be calculated using the following formula, where a is the length of the two equal sides, and b is the base:
Area = 0.5 * b * √(a2-(b/2)2)

The area A of an isosceles triangle can be calculated using the following formula, where a is the length of the two equal sides, and b is the base:
Perimeter = 2a + b

Scalene triangle

A scalene triangle is a type of triangle where all three sides have different lengths, and all three interior angles are also different. Unlike equilateral or isosceles triangles, scalene triangles have no equal sides or angles. This lack of symmetry makes scalene triangles one of the most general forms of triangles in geometry.
a = First side
b = Second side
c = Third side
s = the semi-perimeter

The perimeter P of a scalene triangle is the sum of the lengths of all its sides. If the sides of the triangle are a, b, and c, then the perimeter is calculated using the formula:
Perimeter = a + b + c

The area is calculated using the following formula:
Area = √(s * (s-a) * (s-b) * (s-c))

Right triangle

A right triangle is a type of triangle where one of the angles is exactly 90 degrees. The side opposite the right angle is called the hypotenuse, while the other two sides are known as the legs. The hypotenuse is always the longest side in a right triangle
a = First side
b = Second side
c = hypotenuse

The formula will automatically find the hypotenuse.
The formula for the perimeter is:
Perimeter = a + b + c

To find the area of a right triangle, you use the lengths of the two legs. The formula for the area is:
Area = 0.5 * a * b
